Player Characters

To create a character, roll your ability scores, choose your hometown, determine your background, select a number of techniques equal to your level, and pick your skills.

Ability Scores

A character has the following stats:
> Strength (STR). Your physical fitness and constitution.
> Dexterity (DEX). Your agility and reflexes.
> Awareness (AWR). Your perception and intuition.
> Willpower (WIL). Your mental fortitude and force of personality.

Roll 2d4 - 4 to determine each stat in order.

You can reroll one stat or swap two stats.

If the sum of all four scores is a negative number, start over.

Armor Rating

You ignore a number of points equal to your armor rating (AR) each round of combat. Your base AR (before armor) is equal to your DEX (minimum 0) unless otherwise noted.

Minimum

A character with an ability score of -5 or less in any stat is unplayable, due either to death or madness.

Hometown

Pick one when you create a character.

Aagoda

P 350,000 R Solissar
For reasons only loosely understood, roughly 1 in 10,000 children are born a Noble Solissan — all clones of an ancient Sorceress. These children are adopted and raised by the Solissan Dynasty.

Godsroot

P 1,000,000 R Celestial Plains
Thousands of pilgrims make their way to the Celestial City each year to gaze upon the Holy Mountain. Built on sacred gound at the center of the cosmos, where the Traid fought Tiamat and freed the spheres of her tyrrany.

Ndonec

P 500,000 R Koss
The City of Ten-Thousand Stairs has far more than ten-thousand stairs. Built on and into a mountain of sheer faces and pointed peaks, Ndonec is the largest city in Koss and a major center of trade.

Small Town

P < 5,000 R Koss, Solissar, or the Celestial Plains
Any of a number of quiet settlements, where folk lead a simple life.

Background

Pick two when you create a character.

Burglar

You start with a bag of stolen jewels [1, 3d10 × 10g]. A bounty offering 5× the value of the jewels has been posted for your arrest.

Child of Orcos

Also known as Orcs, some believe Children of Orcos to be born to parents who have broken a sacred oath. You have stonelike skin covered in gnarled horns. You are abnormally tall with pronounced canine teeth or even small tusks. You have +5 HP. Most people will be wary of you.

Clairvoyant

You've had premonitions of the future. Roll 1d20 five times and write down the results on your charater sheet. At any point during the campaign, you can replace a die roll you see with one of the results you recieved. Once a result is used, erase it from your character sheet.

Cultist

You left a dangerous cult. You start with an artifact [1, 20g] that is very important to the cult.

Deserter

You start with a shield [1, 30g, +2 AR, held]. It bears the crest of a military force that you abandoned without leave.

Eavesdropper

You heard something you shouldn't have, know something someone important doesn't want you to know. They know you know.

Fool

You start with an item from the list below.
1 1d20 magic beans [bundle 100, 0c]
2 magic lamp [1, 0c]
3 magic hat [1, 0c, -, head]
4 magic duck (untrained)

You traded a valuable item for it. It has no apparent value or use.

Homunculus

You have 1d4 spell dice and know one of the following spells, determined randomly from the list below. You may only cast this spell with yourself as the target.
1 douse
2 heal
3 indurate
4 lightning rod

You were created artificially and are infertile.

Hermit

You lived in the wilderness far from civilization. You start with a loyal animal companion from the list below.
1 goat
2 boar
3 wolf
4 snake
5 owl
6 frog


Local Hero

You start with a pelt [1, 20g, +1 AR, affixed]. The pelt is from a beast you slayed in defense of a small town or locale.

Orphan

Someone killed your parents. You seek vengeance. Once per day, when you take damage that would reduce you to 0 or less HP, you may instead drop to 1 HP so long as you have not yet enacted vengeance.

Pilgrim

You start with a walking stick [1, 1g, 1d1 blunt, 5'] and a votive [1, 20g] dedicated openly to one of the Triad (or discreetly to one of the Latent Divinities). You are traveling to a sacred site.

Ruin Explorer

You start with an artifact determined randomly from the list below. You found it in an ancient ruin in stunnigly good condition.
1 spear [1, 25g, 1d6 stab, 5', throw 30’, versatile 1d8]
2 helmet [2, 40g, +2 AR, head]
3 mysterious cube [1, 50g]


Scion

You are the descendant of a proud clan and are expected to hold yourself to a specific standard of conduct. If you are in good standing with your clan, you have +2 to Prestige. If you are in bad standing with your clan, you have -2 Prestige. You are currently in neutral standing with your clan.

Squire

You start with a well-made weapon that is worth 20g. The weapon belongs to the knight you serve (or served).

Techniques

Pick one whenever you gain a level. Each time you pick a technique that grants spell dice, you learn a spell from that technique's spell list.

Each technique has a number of keys (A, B, C, and so on). Each successive time you take a technique, you gain the ability associated with the next key, in alphabetical order.

Arsonist

HP +2 SD 5d8
A You add your AWR to all fire damage rolls you make while you are holding a torch or other flaming instrument.
Spells fire plume

Assassin

HP +6
A You deal an additional 1d6 damage to characters you are hidden from. You may use half of your movement on your turn in combat to attempt to hide.
B+ You deal an additional 1d6 damage to characters you are hidden from.


Brawler

HP +6
A Your fists deal 1d8 damage.
B+ The damage die for your fists increases by 1 size.


Cleaver

HP +6
A Whenever an attack you make with an axe causes a character to fall prone, you may make another attack with that axe. The initiative for the extra attack is added to the initiative of the initial attack.


Dual-Wielder

HP +6
A While wielding a weapon in each hand, you may make one attack action with each weapon per round. The initiative for the weapon in your dominant hand is added to the initiative of the weapon in your offhand. The weapon in your offhand cannot have an initiative die greater than the weapon in your dominant hand.


Earthbreaker

HP +6
A You add your initiative to damage you deal with weapons with the multi-handed quality.
B You can wield weapons with the multi-handed 2 quality as though they did not have that quality.


Enchanter

HP +4 SD 3d6
A Whenever you cast a spell that targets a weapon, you may immediately make a weapon attack.
Spells galvanize, serrate

Fortress

HP +6
A You gain an additional +1 AR for each piece of armor you are wearing with the heavy quality.


Gravecaller

HP +2 SD 6d6
A Whenever an undead within 120' of you is reduced to 0 or less HP, you can spend your next action and a number of spell dice less than or equal to your level to heal it. An undead healed in this way acts as though it was animated by you.
Spells animate skeleton, exequy, mummify, necromancy

Houndsmaster

HP +6
A You deal an additional 1d6 damage against targets within 5' of a canine that is allied with you.


Lightning Eater

HP +4 SD 4d4
A Whenever you take lighting damage, you gain SD equal to the damage dice of the attack. Dice gained in this way are not limited by your max spell dice. These dice disappear after a long rest.
Spells galvanize, lightning bolt, lightning rod

Mage

HP +0 SD 9d6
A+ You learn an additional spell from the Mage spell list.
Spells any

Regenerator

HP +2 SD 6d6
A Whenever you cast a spell that causes you to regain hit points, you gain temporary hit points equal to any excess hit points you recover beyond your HP maximum.
B Whenever you cast a healing spell that targets another character, you are healed half the amount the target is healed.
Spells heal, invigorating mist

Slayer

HP +6
A When you attack a character, you may attack recklessly, dealing an additional 2d6 damage to their AR. If you do, you take an additional 1d6 damage to your AR when hit until the beginning of your next turn.


Sniper

HP +6
A When you hit a character with an attack, you deal an additional 1d4 damage for every 40ft space between you and your target.
B You do not deal half damage when firing a weapon with the ranged quality beyond its maximum range.


Spellblade

HP +4 SD 4d4
A Once per round, when you hit a character with a weapon attack, you may immediately cast one spell from the Spellblade spell list. The spell must target the character you hit, but you may ignore the range of the spell.
Spells blood to flame, douse, shadow step

Thrower

HP +6
A Whenever you throw a weapon with the throw quality, you deal damage as if its damage die were one size larger.
B Weapons you wield without the throw quality have the throw 20' quality.


Hit Points

Before taking any techniques, a character has 10 hit points (HP). Each time you take a technique, add the relevant bonus listed in the technique.

Spell Dice

Whenever you take a technique with spell dice (SP), you add the listed dice to your spell dice pool. You can use dice from your pool to cast spells, at which point the dice are expended. All spell dice are added back into the pool after a long rest.

Any character with unexpended spell dice can cast a spell they know. A character cannot spend a number of spell dice greater than their level when casting a spell.

Spells

Each spell lists how many spell dice are required to cast it in square brackets []. If a spell has a + in the square brackets, it can be optionally cast with additional spell dice to highten the effect, as described in the spell description.

Animate Skeleton [1+]

R 5' T skeleton D indefinite
You imbue the target with a facsimile of life, creating an animated skeleton with [dice] + WIL HP. The skeleton will wander aimlessly and attack anything other than you that breathes. It can wield any weapons it wielded in life.

Blood to Flame [1+]

R 30' T character D immediate
Target takes [dice] fire damage. The spell fails if the target does not currently have the bleed condition.

Charm [1+]

R 20' T character D [dice] hrs
Target makes an AWR vs WIL saving throw. If they fail, they regard you as a good friend and ignore the obvious spell you just cast on them. If you spend at least 9 dice to cast this spell, the duration becomes permanent.

Douse [1+]

R 30' T character D [dice] rounds
Target must succeed a DEX vs AWR saving throw or become drenched for the duration.

Exequy [1+]

R 5' T corpse or undead D immediate
You ease the target's soul into the beyond. If the target is undead, they must succeed a WIL saving throw vs. WIL or take [dice] + WIL damage. They take half as much on a successful save. If the target is a corpse, they take [dice] damage upon being risen as an undead, no save.

Fire Plume [1+]

R 60' T character or object D immediate
Deals [dice] + AWR fire damage to the target.

Galvanize [1+]

R 5' T weapon D 1 round
Target weapon deals an additional [dice] lightning damage for the duration.

Heal [1+]

R 5' T character D immediate
Target regains [dice] + WIL HP.

Indurate [1+]

R 5' T character D [dice] rounds
Target's skin hardens and they gain +[num dice] AR for the duration.

Invigorating Mist [1+]

R 30' T [num dice] × 5’ radius D immediate
All characters in the target area regain [num dice] + WIL HP.

Lighting Bolt [1+]

R 60' T character or object D immediate
Deals [dice] + AWR lightning damage to the target.

Lighting Rod [2]

R - T self D 1 round
Redirect lighting effects within 60' toward yourself until the beginning of your next turn.

Mummify [1+]

R 5' T corpse, recently dead D indefinite
Requires embalming materials worth 50g for each spell die used. These materials are expended when the spell is cast.
Takes a number of days equal to the number of spell dice. Target is mummified. If you spend at least 4 spell dice, you can retain one of the body's animal souls. You can retain one extra animal soul for each 2 additional spell die you spend over 4. If you spend at least 8 spell dice, you can retain one of the higher souls. You can retain one extra higher soul for each 2 additional spell die you spend over 8. The mummy's HD is equal to [dice] + WIL.

Animal Souls
> Loyalty. The mummy will follow you and considers you — and anyone else you designate — an ally.
> Aggression. The mummy will attack nearby characters that it does not perceive as an ally.

Higher Souls
> Ingenuity. The mummy can use tools and weapons.
> Speech. The mummy can speak (but retains little knowledge of its former life).
> Labor. The mummy can perform simple tasks (like picking up groceries or burying a body). It will attempt to perform these tasks without tools if it can't use them.


Necromancy [1+]

R 5' T corpse D indefinite
Target will answer [num dice] questions. The corpse is only working with what it knew in life, and may lie to you if it would've done so in life.

Serrate [1+]

R - T weapon D 1 round
Target weapon has bleed [dice] for the duration.

Summon [1+]

R 5' T open space D immediate

You summon a creature you've made a contract with or an item you've marked.

Shadow Step [1+]

R 60' T character D immediate
You teleport to a space within 5' × [num dice] of the target.

Skills

You start with 6 skill points (in addition to any you get from your hometown), which you can distribute however you like among 2-6 skills. A skill you invest 1 point in becomes a +1 skill,and so on.

You may choose your skills from the list of example skills below or make your own. As a general guideline, anything that could conceivably be a college major makes a good skill. If you make your own, they must be approved by your DM before play.
